What is Marketing?

Understanding the Core Concept

Definition

Marketing is a process by which companies create value for customers and build strong customer relationships in order to capture value from customers in return.
üí° Key Insight: Marketing is not just about selling products - it's about creating genuine value for customers and building lasting relationships.

AMA's Formal Definition

According to the American Marketing Association:

"Marketing is the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large."

Marketing is Everywhere

Marketing surrounds us in various forms:

Traditional Forms:

TV commercials, print ads, billboards, radio spots

Digital Forms:

Websites, mobile apps, social media, online videos, email campaigns

The Marketing Process

Creating and Capturing Customer Value

Five-Step Marketing Process

Step 1

Understand the marketplace and customer needs

Step 2

Design a customer value-driven marketing strategy

Step 3

Construct an integrated marketing program

Step 4

Engage customers and build relationships

Step 5

Capture value from customers

üìå Important: The first four steps create value FOR customers. The final step captures value FROM customers in return.

Understanding Customer Needs

The Foundation of Marketing

Needs, Wants, and Demands

Needs

States of felt deprivation (physical, social, individual)

Wants

Form that needs take, shaped by culture and personality

Demands

Wants backed by buying power

Market Offerings

Combinations of products, services, information, or experiences offered to satisfy needs or wants.

Example: Airbnb

CEO Brian Chesky and co-founder Joe Gebbia regularly stay at host locations to understand real user experiences and shape new customer solutions.

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoiding Marketing Myopia

Marketing Myopia: Paying more attention to specific products than to the benefits and experiences they produce.

Example: Railroad companies declined because they saw themselves in the "railroad business" rather than the "transportation business."

Value Proposition

Creating Competitive Advantage

What is a Value Proposition?

The set of benefits or values a company promises to deliver to customers to satisfy their needs.

Examples of Strong Value Propositions

Sonos One with Alexa

"The smart speaker for music lovers" - All the advantages of Alexa but with high-quality Sonos sound

Amazon

"Earth's most customer-centric company" - Creating genuine value through convenience, selection, and service

Jeni's Splendid Ice Creams

"Ice creams created in fellowship with growers, makers, and producers" - Artisanal quality and community values

Building Customer Relationships

Creating Long-term Value

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

The overall process of building and maintaining profitable customer relationships by delivering superior customer value and satisfaction.

Customer-Perceived Value

The difference between total customer benefits and customer costs

Customer Satisfaction

The extent to which perceived performance matches expectations

Customer Engagement Marketing

Fostering direct and continuous customer involvement in shaping brand conversations, experiences, and community.

Example: BarkBox

Rather than hard-sell pitches, Bark engages customers with humor about "the weird dogs we live with and the funny things they do."

Key Metrics

Customer Lifetime Value

Value of entire stream of purchases over a lifetime

Share of Customer

Portion of customer's purchasing in product categories

Customer Equity

Total combined lifetime values of all customers
